This is the first post, so let me say plainly what this space is for.

For years I've held a stubborn intuition: that the field took a wrong turn when it
started measuring intelligence by benchmarks and growing it by brute scale. The
brain — the most complicated biochemical system we know — learns continuously,
knows something about its own limits, and runs on roughly twenty watts. None of
the systems we celebrate today do all three.

So this blog is where I'll think out loud about the alternatives: open-ended
systems that set their own goals, agents that model themselves, and architectures
small and efficient enough to be responsible to the planet they run on. Some posts
will be half-formed. That's the point — writing is how I'll turn intuitions into
something precise enough to test.

## How this works

Every post here is just a Markdown file. To publish a new one, I add a file to
`src/content/blog/`, write, and push. That's the whole workflow.
